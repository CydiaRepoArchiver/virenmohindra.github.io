Our Story


Girls for Tomorrow (GFT) was born of the mute eloquence of a young woman who singlehandedly shook the entire nation to stand against the fight for women's rights. The 2012 Delhi rape case horrified the world and drew attention to gender-based violence, and the victim became known as “Nirbhaya” – now a household name for courage and valor in India. Feeling inspired to contribute to mobilizing the societal change required for women to enjoy safe and strong future, we founded Girls for Tomorrow to lionize womanhood. Wrapped up in our own worlds, we often forget what it means to be a woman, and instead simply accept the challenges and occasional dangers around us. We believe that change begins by changing one idea at a time: starting from our own neighborhood, area, city, state and maybe whole nation eventually.

When we began this initiative in 2013, my sister and I hoped to make a small difference in our immediate radius, specifically among school-going girls. Over the past two years our story has grown to include many more chapters and many more people whose lives have been touched. Our mission is to spread awareness of physical fitness and well-being for girls by imparting them with the right knowledge and skills. Furthermore, we want to support and counsel them on other aspects that make a girl strong and self-reliant. GFT was initiated on the notion that today self defense is an essential life skill — for girls and woman across the globe. So as Girls for Tomorrow , we will channel our energy to help grow strong women – one girl at a time.

A Girl for Tomorrow will be fearless, confident and self empowered. She is strong and independent on her own; our goal is to support and celebrate her!

ANYA & ESHA BHARADWAJ

Girls For Tomorrow

Create awareness and inspire change through active participation of the youth to combat gender based violence in our country.

What we do?

Our modules are simple: We conduct one day camps for schools and weekly workshops for neighbourhoods. These are conducted by trained martial arts teachers who are specialised in Self Defense combatting. These sessions are typically a mix of martial and yoga techniques. The workshop consists of modules on awareness camps, sex education, counselling, self-defence, rescue operations, offensive combat and escape methods.

Volunteer

Since we depend on local volunteers, we look for representatives to carry out this initiative forward in different regions. We continuously need volunteers to help us in spreading the awareness through these workshops. So come join us if you are willing and spread a positive attitude.

Attend a workshop

Be a part of any of our weekly workshops or one day camps. Listings for our upcoming events will be available on our website. Alternatively you could write to us at contactus.gft@gmail.com

GFT in your neighbourhood

Having a passion for the cause is all you need to start a chapter. We help you in identifying and initiating a local chapter. This entails regularly conducting workshops and giving us updates on your chapter. Write to us about your proposal and we will work out a plan best suited.

Share your Story

Send in your story (names kept anonymous) of any incident or inspiration that you encountered. Tell us in what way it changed you and how you plan on taking it forward. Sharing will help us in strengthening our cause for a better tomorrow.

Share Ideas

We are all part of the same youth seeking a change. Inputs and ideas from others are very valuable to us as they are the key to taking this initiative forward regionally. So please send us your thoughts and be a partner of GFT.

GFT Workshops

Art Bazaar: Building Awareness

Oct 2014, Delhi
Fund raiser painting exhibition made by volunteers on 12x12 inch canvas themed 'Tree of Life' for local Diwali Bazaar. These funds were used for conducting weekly workshops.

Weekly GFT Camp

Sessions since Dec 2013, Delhi
A weekly workshop for girls (12+) who come together in Vasant Kunj not only to learn self defence techniques such as martial arts but also to share their experiences. These are conducted by our professional mentors.

Train the Trainer Camp, Red Brigade

October 2013, Lucknow
A camp attended by our trainers to impart skills and give them first hand training to conduct Self Defense classes. Usha Vishwakarma of Red Brigade, a survivor and an instructor for combatting gender based violence, conducted this workshop.

Nishchay Jaipur Camp

May 2014, Jaipur


Nischay with a strength of 500 girls aims at provide educational and vocational skills to girls below the poverty line to empower them with the dignity of life. GFT organised one day awareness camp for them in Rajasthan where basic survival skills were demonstrated.

Yoga Connect

Feb 2014, Delhi
Yoga camp conducted by Anya and Esha (trained Yoga teachers) to learn importance of maintaining mental and physical balance through ancient art of yoga.

GFT in Madhya Pradesh:

Feb 2015, Chhattarpur
A new chapter opened to the girls of Chhattarpur, Madhya Pradesh, where our Volunteers took the task of spreading awareness in rural India. They tied up with a local school where they conducted workshops.

Adult Awareness Camp

August 2014, Delhi
One day workshops designed especially for teachers in South Delhi schools to create awareness for Self Defence. The workshop resulted in teachers actively introducing and creating awareness among the students of their class.

Udayan School , Kolkatta

April 2015, Kokatta
A talk on the importance of self defence by Mahima, our Kolkatta founder and a TaeKwonDo workshop by Mr. Mrityunjoy Roy was conducted for the children of Udayan, a home for the children of leprosy sufferers in Barrackpore.
